About TextLens

Built for Anyone Who Needs Text
From an Image

TextLens was created with a single mission: to give everyone a fast, free, and completely private way to extract text from images — without installing software or creating accounts.

Our Mission

Text is often trapped inside images — screenshots, scanned receipts, photographs of signs, PDFs converted to images, and more. TextLens exists to set that text free. We believe OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology should be accessible to everyone, not locked behind paywalls or complex software installations.

Every feature we build is guided by three core principles: it must be fast, private, and free. We never compromise on any of these.

How TextLens Works

TextLens is powered by Tesseract.js, a JavaScript port of Google's industry-leading Tesseract OCR engine. When you upload an image, Tesseract.js runs entirely within your browser using WebAssembly — a near-native execution environment built into modern browsers.

This means your image never leaves your device. There is no server-side processing, no file storage, and no data collection of any kind. The entire OCR pipeline — from image loading to text output — happens locally on your computer or phone.

Who Uses TextLens?

TextLens is used by a wide range of people for many everyday tasks:

  • Students — Digitizing handwritten or printed notes from textbook photos.
  • Professionals — Extracting text from scanned documents, business cards, or meeting whiteboards.
  • Writers & Researchers — Converting image-based quotes or references into editable text for citations.
  • Developers — Quickly extracting error messages from screenshots without retyping.
  • Anyone — Anyone who has ever needed to copy text from an image and couldn't.

Technology Stack

TextLens is built with simplicity and performance in mind. There is no heavyweight framework, no server infrastructure, and no unnecessary dependencies:

  • HTML5 — Semantic, accessible markup for all pages.
  • CSS3 — Custom properties, responsive grid, smooth animations.
  • Vanilla JavaScript — Zero dependencies beyond Tesseract.js.
  • Tesseract.js v5 — The browser-native OCR engine powering text extraction.
  • WebAssembly — Used internally by Tesseract.js for near-native performance.
